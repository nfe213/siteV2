TOM:UA Tennis Ball Cutter

Project Manager

  • Led a student engineering team in designing a 3D Printed tool for the elderly to safely, cheaply, and independently cut tennis balls for the feet of mobility walkers.
  • This tool was laters used by teachers who wanted to put tennis balls on the bottom of chairs in their classrooms.
  • The files used to create this tool, along with instructions for assembly will be uploaded to the TOM Global website and other file sharing sites so it can be recreated by other TOM Communities around the world.

  • Modified IKEA Lack coffee table to increase rigidity and minimize vibrations during printing.
  • Added all-metal hotend, fillament runout sensor, and auto-bed-leveling to decrease manual involvment in printing operations.
